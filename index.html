Essence–Possibility Theory

A Logical and Metaphysical Framework by Seyed HamidReza Reyhani

Date: November 11, 2025   •   Author: Seyed HamidReza Reyhani   •   Affiliation: Founder of OpenLogic

Overview

Essence–Possibility Theory proposes a unified framework that connects essence (the core defining properties of entities) with possibility (the capacity for realization). It asserts that impossibility eliminates essence — meaning that entities or events that cannot possibly exist lack definable properties or nature.

Developed under the OpenLogic Initiative, this theory refines classical metaphysics, logic, and modal reasoning by formalizing the dependency of essence on possibility.

Core Principle

Possibility as the Gateway to Essence

Without the potential to exist or occur, no entity can meaningfully possess essence.

Formally:

¬◊P → ¬∃Ess(P)

If proposition P is impossible, then no set of essential properties (Ess(P)) exists for it.

Logical Framework

Using standard modal logic (systems K, T, S4, S5):

ConceptSymbolMeaning
Necessity□PP is true in all possible worlds
Possibility◊PP is true in at least one possible world
Core Axiom¬◊P → ¬∃Ess(P)Impossibility negates essence

Duality Relations

  • ◊P ⟺ ¬□¬P
  • □P ⟺ ¬◊¬P

Proof Sketch

  1. Essence implies essential properties (essentialism).
  2. Essential properties require at least one possible world.
  3. Therefore, if no possible world allows P (¬◊P), P has no essence.

Implications

Philosophy

Refines essentialism by rejecting the notion of “impossible essences.” Prevents ontological inflation with logically inconsistent entities (e.g., “square circle”).

Logic & AI

Helps reasoning systems discard infeasible states. Improves inference efficiency and conceptual clarity in symbolic AI.

Physics

Supports theoretical boundaries by identifying phenomena without realizable essence (e.g., perpetual motion).

Relation to Other Theories

Essence–Possibility Theory complements Alternative Perspective Theory by addressing the qualitative essence of what can exist, while APT addresses multi-perspectival reality modeling. Together, they form a layered metaphysical framework for existence and possibility.
